Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Strategic Plan

Carnegie Mellon University and the Dietrich College for Humanities and Social Sciences value a dynamic and diverse community where all members are responsible for contributing to an inclusive environment. The Dietrich College values diversity, equity and inclusion because:

  • diverse institutions function better than homogenous ones; and
  • equal opportunity for all is a moral imperative.
The college strives to promote a culture of respect, scholarship, innovation and integrity as we advance talented minds from diverse backgrounds. We aim to achieve these goals by implementing concrete practices at the center of our daily work that minimize bias and support diversity, by holding ourselves accountable to show progress and by attending to and revising these practices relentlessly.

Goal: Recruit and Retain a Diverse Student Body
Initiatives

Diversity@Dietrich is a series of DEI events and trainings created to improve the reach of DEI programs and to directly address undergraduate student requests for more education and training resources to improve student, staff and faculty awareness and acceptance of our diverse student-base.

Dietrich Academy is a summer pre-college program for low-income, first-generation, and other students from the Pittsburgh area who contribute to diversity. This program aims to connect these students to each other fostering a strong cohort, and to encourage them to identify with and apply to Dietrich College.

Dietrich College is providing equitable financial support to expand PhD Fellowships to support one program participant per year per department. Existing Dietrich College fellowships are being repurposed so that instead of each unit receiving a small sum annually, the college uses these funds to bootstrap college-wide fellowships for PhDs who contribute to the College’s diversity.

The Dietrich College Fifth Year Master’s Bridge Program is a one-year bridge-to-PhD post-baccalaureate or master’s program for students who contiibute o the College’s diversity.
Goal: Provide an equitable and inclusive environment for students.
Initiatives

The Dietrich College Cultural Climate Survey will be a regularly administered survey which specifically assessses diversity, inclusion, equity and access issues that impact Dietrich undergraduate students.

Faculty mentorship training will be instituted to all faculty advisors that are specific to the needs of first-generation graduate students and those from under-represented populations.

The Dietrich Annual Town Hall will be hosted by the Dean's office to offer an opportunity for Dietrich College students to raise graduate-specific concerns, acting as a mechanism for feedback and redress of grievances.

Goal: Improve Acess and Equity for Historically Underrepresented Minorities and Women Faculty
Initiatives

Creation of new hiring practices is a multi-step process starting with each department developing a departmental diversity recruitment plan to adress specific conditions and goals. Along side the recruitment plan will be the creation of a hiring procedures checklist at the college level, development of aspirational goals and search approvals, creation of search training, and lastly development of hiring process accountability.

  1. Identify Resources to Attract Canidates

    2. Associate Dean will help secure resources such as space, start-up packages, daycare spots, etc. to maximize prospect of offer acceptance. Both, the dean and Assosciate dean will help departments applying for the newly created Faculty Target of Oppourtunity Find to hire faculty that contribute to CMU's mission of DEI.

  2. Expand Presidental Post-doctoral Fellows Program to Dietrich Fellows

    3. Dean will continue to contribute resources to a general pool from which post-doctoral fellows can be partially subsidized as well also offer tailored supplemental programming to focus Dietrich College recruiting efforts.

  3. Create new positions

    4. New positions will increase DEI in research and education within Dietrich. This will be accomplished using the Faculty Target of Opportunity Fund.

  1. Develop Leadership Training

    2. Use of a Promotion and Tenure Checklist can be used by department chairs to ensure consistency in practices and timing of feedback.

  2. Create Faculty Workshops

    3. Workshops will offer insight about how to develop a complelling portfolio of research, service, and teaching through a mock evaluation to educate junior faculty on the process.

  1. Develop mentorship program

    2. Pairing untenured faculty with departmental mentors, junior faculty can obtain advice on strategic planning or research, as well as developing a teaching and professional portfolio.

  2. Identify sponsors

    3. Faculty will receive training on the differences between mentoring and sponsoring female faculty.

  3. Create grant writing mentorship program

    4. Junior faculty will be paired with successful senior faculty grant recipients in the departments to help facilitate and support grant writing and identification.

  4. Identify resources to retain faculty

    5. Associate dean for faculty will coordinate efforts to secure resources such as space, resources for research, daycare spots, etc. to retain faculty. Also, associate dean will work with CMU’s alumni networks and our Office of Corporate Relations for help with spousal hiring.

  5. Create an ombudsperson committee

    6. Faculty Senate will develop a small ombudsperson committee (3–4 faculty) who can work with individual faculty who face problems within in their departments, and act as intermedaries to help triage situations.

Departments will track the service activities and teaching assignments to make sure that these activities are equitable across faculty.

Coordinate with University on on-going policy development to ensure salary equity for faculty.
Goal: Ensure a multicultural and inclusive faculty climate
Initiatives

  1. Create reporting mechanisms

    2. Once a semester, the dean will report on the status of Dietrich College’s DEI initiatives.

  2. Ensure transparency

    3. Develop transparent search procedures for Dietrich leadership positions and faculty chairs.

  3. Establish DEI Awards

    4. Establish awards and recognition for faculty involved in DEI activities. Awards will be given at graduation ceremonies or other Dietrich College events.

  4. Ensure diversity is visible in event and seminar series roles

    5. Make sure that there is diversity in college and department-level events, such as conferences, seminar series, events, graduation and other community events.

  5. Standardize messaging and communications

    6. Dietrich leadership will reinforce the DEI agenda through communications sent to students, alumni, donors, faculty, staff and other constituents.

Opportunities include future series of talks/ conference that highlights current DEI issues or diversity and inclusion training modfules for faculty.
Goal: Provide support for conducting DEI research
Initiatives

  1. Prioritize fundraising for research on diversity, equity and inclusion

    2. Dietrich College will seek out resources that can provide funding for research initiatives, centers and labs

  2. Create cluster hires

    3. Create a new Dietrich research thrust in the area of diversity, equity and inclusion to attract diverse faculty members.

  3. Create a visiting diversity scholars program

    4. Provide support for visiting scholars, for initiatives such as CAUSE and D3 that support our diversity, equity and inclusion strategic efforts.

  4. Create an initiative on diversity, equity and inclusion research

    5. Dietrich will launch an initiative (potentially in coordination with cluster hires) or extend current initiatives (CAUSE and D3) to conduct research on DEI.

  5. Provide resources for research funding

    6. Start a fund to seed projects in the area of DEI, as well as provide money for diversity-related conferences, lectures and campus events.

  6. Report on research activity in departments

    7. Faculty annual reports should include information on research conducted on DEI. Report should include published papers, conference presentations, events, funding applied for and received and graduate/ undergraduate students supported who are involved in the research.

Goal: Enhance teaching and diversity
Initiatives

  1. Provide training to create diversity content in courses

    2. Develop a training guide for faculty on how to include DEI issues into courses. Would contain a list of approaches to accomplish this goal, and workshops can be developed in collaboration with the Eberly Center.

  2. Develop Dietrich curriculum

    3. Ensure that the some of the Grand Challenge Seminars are focused around DEI issues. In addition, courses on intercultural and global learning can focus on topics that attend directly to concepts and intellectual framing of issues pertaining to diversity and inclusion.

  3. Create diversity teaching awards

    4. Create two annual awards for Dietrich College faculty. One award will recognize an exceptional exemplar of infusing DEI into the curriculum. The other will recognize exceptional use of inclusive classroom practices.

  4. Annotate diversity perspectives in courses in annual reports
  5. Incentivize course improvements

    6. Small fund should be established for faculty who are re-vamping their courses to enhance the focus on DEI.

Eberly Center faculty and the Dietrich College director of diversity and inclusion can offer workshops on ways to increase inclusivity and a sense of belonging in the classroom. Additionally, to ensure accountability, Dietrich will add a question on the faculty course evaluations to assess instructor’s ability to create an inclusive classroom environment.

Goal: Build and foster a positive work environment for staff in Dietrich College
Initiatives

Creation of previously mentioned Climate Survey will help ascertain experiences of staff. Additionally, CMU Human Resources has developed new resources for on-boarding new employees and established a working group to develop training programs to expand new hires’ understanding of DEI as well as racism and bias. HR also plans to establish a process of exit interviews that include a survey and guidance documents to better understand the reasons why employees separate from the university

Establish the Dietrich Staff Awards and have a celebration to recognize the award recipients, which is attended by the college leadership and department heads. We would also like to see staff figures represented on the websites for both the college and university.

  • Established the Staff Supervisory Awareness Program to provide all supervisors at the Uinversity with a solid foundation of core human resources knowledge critical to their success
  • Launched a pilot CMULead program for staff aimed at fostering their development as leaders, expanding their networks and encouraging their stewardship of the university’s mission, vision and strategic goals
  • Create a fund that allows staff to participate in external conferences and executive education programs provided on campus at no additional cost

Develop standing committee that will have equal representation of faculty, staff, undergraduate and graduate students, with two year appointments to ensure continuity of work. Participation in this committee should be factored into tenure and promotion decisions for faculty and staff respectively.
Goal: Recruit, retain and cultivate diversity with our staff
Initiatives

Dietrich is going to review job descriptions, develop a repository of partners to market open positions, and establish a clear onboarding practice for new staff. Additioanlly, CMU has partnered with Vibrant Pittsburgh, an economic development nonprofit with the mission to build a thriving and inclusive region by attracting, retaining and elevating a diverse pool of talent.
